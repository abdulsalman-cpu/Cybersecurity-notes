Day 1 – Cybersecurity Basics

---

## 1 - What is Cybersecurity
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, systems, networks, and data from attacks or unauthorized access.

---

## 2 - CIA Triad
The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity. It focuses on three main principles:

### (C) Confidentiality
Keeping secrets safe. Only authorized people should access the data.

### (I) Integrity
Making sure data is accurate and has not been changed without permission.

### (A) Availability
Making sure systems and data are available whenever needed.

---

## 3 - The Danger: V.T.R Formula
Vulnerability + Threat = Risk

### Vulnerability
A weakness in a system (like a hole in a fence) that can be exploited.

### Threat
Something that can take advantage of a vulnerability such as a hacker, malware, or natural event.

### Risk
The chance that something bad will actually happen when a threat uses a vulnerability.

---

## 4 - When Security Fails: The DAD Result
If security fails, the opposite of the CIA Triad can happen.

### Disclosure
Secrets get exposed or leaked. Opposite of confidentiality.

### Alteration
Data is changed without permission. Opposite of integrity.

### Destruction
Data or systems are damaged or deleted.

---

## Final Key Idea
Cybersecurity is about protecting data using CIA, understanding attacks using VTR, and knowing failure outcomes using DAD.
